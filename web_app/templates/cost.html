💡 Key Economic Insights
  • Feedstock is the dominant cost driver (67.6%). Developing low-cost substrates or integrating waste streams is critical for cost reduction.
  • Scale is crucial: A 10% increase in annual production reduces LCOH by ~$0.25/kg, almost 10% of total cost.
  • Utilities are the second-largest cost (21.5%), mainly from pH buffering and aeration. Energy recovery could improve economics.
  • Capital represents only 7.3% of LCOH over a 20-year plant life with 8% discount rate. Optimizing feed control (MPC) is more valuable than saving on reactors.
  • At ${{ "%.2f"|format(data.summary.get('LCOH ($/kg H2)', 0)) }}/kg H₂: Still above current SMR hydrogen (~$1.50/kg), but competitive with PEM electrolysis + renewable grid (target: $2–3/kg by 2030).
