🧬 ADM1 State Variables & Key Indicators

The Anaerobic Digestion Model No. 1 (ADM1) tracks 19+ state variables representing bacterial populations, soluble substrates, particulate substrates, and gas phases. Below are the critical variables for hydrogen production:

🦠 Biomass: X_h2 (H₂-Producing Bacteria)

Acidogenic bacteria that produce H₂ from carbohydrates and amino acids. Growth and decay are driven by substrate availability and pH inhibition (optimal pH 5.0–5.5).

⚛️ Substrate: S_su (Monosaccharides/Glucose)

Readily fermentable sugar. Fed as constant influent; consumed by acidogenic bacteria. High residual indicates excess feeding; depletion limits H₂ production.

💨 Gas: S_h2 (Dissolved H₂)

Dissolved hydrogen in liquid. High levels indicate inhibition by H₂ partial pressure. Gas stripping (sparging/biogas recovery) moves H₂ to gas phase.

🔬 pH Control

ADM1 includes complex acid-base equilibria. In this study, pH is set directly (buffering assumption) to isolate the effect of pH on kinetics without pH solver complexity.

🌡️ Temperature Correction

All rate constants (k_m, Y_*, etc.) are scaled by k(T) = k_ref × exp(E_a/R × (1/T_ref - 1/T)). Typical E_a ≈ 50 kJ/mol for enzymatic processes.

🚫 Methanogenesis Disabled

Rho_11 (acetale → CH₄) and Rho_12 (H₂ + CO₂ → CH₄) are set to zero. This is the key modification that forces H₂ to accumulate instead of being consumed.
